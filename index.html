How Matching Works
The engine runs a Double-Auction per timeslot — sellers sorted by lowest ask, buyers by highest bid.
A match occurs when buyer max price ≥ seller min price. Clearing price = midpoint of the two bounds.
Unmatched surplus → Grida export @ SMP. Unmatched deficit → Grida import @ retail rate.
⚠️
Why Matching Can Fail
① Timeslot mismatch — sellers and buyers active at different hours.
② Price gap — seller min price > buyer max price.
③ Tight tolerance — 0–5% leaves little room to find overlap.
Fix: align hours, lower ask / raise bid, or increase tolerance %.
💡
Multi-Tier Bidding
Submit multiple rows for the same hour at different prices to capture more matches while protecting margin. The cheapest sell tier is matched first.
user1, 14:00, 400Wh, ₩950, 15%  ← cheap tier
user1, 14:00, 300Wh, ₩1150, 10% ← mid tier
user1, 14:00, 150Wh, ₩1380, 5%  ← premium
🔌
Grida Fallback Rates
Set the SMP rate (what Grida pays sellers for surplus) and Retail rate (what Grida charges buyers for deficit) before running matching.
P2P savings are calculated relative to these rates — the wider the gap between SMP and retail, the more valuable P2P trading becomes.

How to read this comparison
The AI session replaces only the trained user's manual bids with ARIMA-predicted values — all other users keep their original manual inputs. A higher AI score means the prediction found better trading opportunities than manual input. A lower AI score is also valid — it means the user's manual strategy was already well-optimized, or the prediction window did not align well with the session. Neither outcome is wrong — this comparison helps each user decide whether AI assistance adds value for their specific trading pattern.
